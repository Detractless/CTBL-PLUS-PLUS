CTBL ++ Setup

Before you begin

CTBL++ is a commitment tool that layers on top of Cold Turkey Blocker. It exists to make your blocks genuinely hard to undo in a moment of weakness, not just inconvenient but deliberately resistant to the usual ways people talk themselves out of focus.

This setup is not a routine install. It will create a dedicated account, register background services, and place real, intentional restrictions on this computer. Several of these changes are difficult to reverse by design.

Read each page carefully. You will be asked to confirm that you understand what is being changed before anything is applied, and later you will complete a few steps yourself so that you remain in control of the process.

Take the next few pages at a readable pace. There is no rush, and nothing is changed until you choose to begin.

How it works

What CTBL++ does

CTBL++ adds three things to a normal Cold Turkey setup. Together they turn an ordinary blocker into a commitment device.

The Queue Delay

Ever unlocked a block and forgotten to lock it back? Or told yourself you'd re-lock it later? Maybe you even thought that you'd have one last "good time" first. Those slips all come from the same thing: Cold Turkey requires a block to be off, at its most vulnerable and easiest point to relapse, just to make a singular change. CTBL++ removes that step. You queue the change and it opens after a delay you have chosen in advance, so there's nothing to re-lock and no instant access for an impulse to latch on to.

The Watchdog Triad

Three background processes monitor each other and the enforcement engine. If one is terminated, the others restart it. This is what keeps the protection running rather than something you can simply close when it becomes inconvenient.

System-level enforcement

CTBL++ also closes the common escape routes (clock changes, tool-based termination, uninstalling, and recovery shortcuts), so that the waiting period actually means something. The next page explains exactly what that involves, because some of it is significant.

What this will do to this PC

These are real, intentional changes. They are what make the tool effective, but you should understand each one before continuing.

!
System tools are blocked. Task Manager, Registry Editor, and similar utilities are prevented from launching while CTBL++ is installed. This stops the engine from being killed manually.
!
The uninstaller is disabled. CTBL++ cannot be removed through the normal Windows uninstall entry. Removal requires the documented uninstall process.
!
Recovery options are restricted. Factory reset and Windows recovery shortcuts are limited, so they can't be used as a quick bypass.
!
Administrator access is sealed off. You set a strong password on the CTBLAdmin account and discard it, then demote your own account to a standard user. CTBLAdmin stays the only administrator, but with its password gone nobody can use it. This PC has no usable admin access, and regaining it is intentionally very difficult.
!
The setup password matters. You will set a password for CTBLAdmin during the final steps. If you lose it before finishing, you may be unable to complete the installation, and recovery is deliberately hard.

Demote your own account in Windows Settings:

  1. Open Settings → Accounts → Other users (called Family & other users on Windows 10).
  2. For every administrator account except CTBLAdmin: select it, click Change account type, choose Standard User, and click OK.
  3. Leave CTBLAdmin as Administrator; do not change it. Its discarded password is what keeps it sealed.

Do not demote CTBLAdmin. Windows needs at least one administrator to run reliably; with none, some things (especially startup applications) can fail or behave unpredictably. CTBLAdmin fills that role while staying locked behind its discarded password, so no one can actually use it.

Log off to finish

The protections take full effect after you sign out. Closing this installer does not sign you out; you must do that yourself.

  1. You may close this installer now.
  2. Open the Start menu, select your account, and choose Sign out.
  3. When you sign back in, CTBL++ is active and sealed.

From this point on, administrator access is gone and the enforcement engine is running. This is the committed state you set up.
